The hidden cause

The smell isn't always coming from what you can brush away.

Bad breath isn't always a sign of poor brushing. In many cases, it's linked to an imbalance of bacteria that naturally live in your mouth.

  • Helpful bacteria work quietly to protect your mouth
  • Odor-causing bacteria release sulfur compounds — the actual source of the smell
  • After brushing, odor-causing bacteria can begin multiplying again within hours

Left: beneficial bacteria  ·  Right: odor-causing bacteria

Why the smell returns

Why brushing isn't fixing the real problem

Brushing cleans surfaces — but it doesn't permanently rebalance the bacteria living underneath. Fresh breath returns for a while, then the odor comes creeping back. You end up treating the symptom, again and again, instead of the source.

A clean surface doesn't mean a sealed source.

So what happens when you focus on the source instead of constantly masking the symptoms?
